Home Energy OS

Writing about a multi-vendor home energy controller built on Home Assistant, Node-RED, a small policy engine, and a drift sentinel. Production install on a residential property in the Pacific Northwest.

  • A Practical Home Energy OS with Home Assistant

    May 2026 · ~4,250 words

    How a FranklinWH whole-home battery, an Enphase solar array, a SPAN panel, three EcoFlow backup batteries, and a Rivian R1T became one coherent controller. Covers data fusion across five vendors, the policy engine that decides what to do with the result, the drift sentinel that catches when the engine and reality disagree, and the unglamorous discipline of refusing to do things.

  • When the Gateway Goes Dark: A First Outage Postmortem

    May 2026 · ~2,100 words · follow-up to the above

    A real power outage tested the controller for the first time and found something the original design didn't account for: the resilience of the resilience system itself. The gateway lost power, came back amnesiac, misclassified the cap edge, and quietly imported electricity from the grid to overcharge the home battery. What the drift sentinel got right, what it couldn't do, and what got shipped in response.

Source for the writeups (and the controller itself) lives on GitHub. Comparing notes welcome — contact via GitHub issues on the repo.
